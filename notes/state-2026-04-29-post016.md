## What changed since the late snapshot (016 findings)

### The methodology arc is closed

014/14b validated synthetic compute (FMA carry-chain, 6.33 ns/FMA);
015 validated synthetic memory (pointer-chase, 341 ns/chained
load); **016 validated transfer to a real ML kernel.** The
foundational arc 001-016 is now an end-to-end pipeline:

```
001-008  →  timing infrastructure & telemetry stack
009-013  →  M4 Max DVFS + sub-floor regime characterized
014-015  →  amplification methodology validated on synthetic both halves
016      →  amplification applied to real-world matmul, signal recovered
```

Per-shape ps/FLOP recoverable for 30/34 shapes with R² ≥ 0.99.
The methodology FAIL flag in analysis.py is technically correct
(5 %-max-residual gate fails on parallelism-floor cells) but
substantively the methodology PASSed in its design regime.

### Naive fp32 matmul on M4 Max — first lab reference numbers

| regime | shapes | ps/FLOP | TFLOP/s |
|--------|--------|--------:|--------:|
| compute plateau | M=N=K ∈ [128, 768] | 0.45-0.54 | 1.86-2.21 |
| over-SLC (mild) | M=N=K=2048 (32 MB)  | 0.87 | 1.15 |
| memory-bound probe | (2, 8192, 4096) | 3.91 | 0.26 |
| ILP-friendly small K | M=N=512, K=2 | 0.148 | 6.78 |

**Naive matmul tops out at ~2 TFLOP/s, ~7 % of fp32 peak**
(28 TFLOP/s at 1.578 GHz). This is the first non-synthetic
compute reference number established in the lab. Any future
tiled / shared-memory / MPS comparison has this as the floor.

### Three discrimination axes work

016 identified three orthogonal discrimination signals on naive
matmul:
- **Size axis**: parallelism floor at M ≤ 32, compute plateau at
  M ∈ [128, 768], mild rise toward SLC-exceeding (M=2048: 1.9×
  plateau).
- **K axis (at fixed M=N)**: smooth compute-to-memory transition
  as K grows. Two effects compound: small K → ILP-friendly
  (no serial-dep on `acc`); large K → cache pressure.
- **Aspect ratio**: narrow output (M ∈ {2, 4, 8} with N=8192,
  K=4096) gives the cleanest memory-bound signal — ps/FLOP scales
  with 1/M as per-thread reuse decreases.

The cleanest single-axis signal is the M=N=512 K-sweep (3.8× rise
from K=2 to K=4096).

### PRFBOOST trigger refined (broader than 015 said)

015 closed PRFBOOST as "sustained memory-bound dispatches with
single-dispatch wall-clock ≥ ~5 ms." 016 shows it also engages
on **compute-bound 128³ matmul** at N_AMP=1024, where one
dispatch is ~2 ms and the workload is compute-dominated. Updated
trigger model: **sustained dispatch activity with high enough
power draw**, not specifically memory-bound. This re-opens the
"is 015's PRFBOOST rule complete?" question; the exact boundary
is open.

### Internal-loop amp's cache-hot bias quantified

Pre-reg predicted internal-loop amp would mute cache-fit signal
for square shapes (because A and B reuse cache after first amp-
iter). Confirmed: square-diagonal cache-fit rise is mild (1.9×
max). To probe true cold-cache memory-bound behavior on naive
matmul, future experiments need either single-dispatch direct
timing (no amp) or aspect-ratio shapes (016's memory-bound probes)
where per-thread reuse is structurally lower regardless of amp.

## Operational rules per chip

### M1 Pro / `applegpu_g13s` (unchanged from late snapshot)

- Dispatch-overhead floor: ~8 µs back-to-back.
- Warmup recipe: `same K=1` (avoid `fma_loop K=1`). Decision 004.
- Work-dominance: write_tid ≥ 131 K-262 K threads, fma_loop ≥ 256.
- **No state-channel telemetry, amplification, PRFBOOST, or
  matmul-discrimination work validated here yet.** Everything
  009-016 is M4 Max only.

### M4 Max / `applegpu_g16s`

#### Timing
- Dispatch-overhead floor: ~6.4 µs cool / ~1.7 µs DEADLINE-mode
  sub-floor. **Both are slow / fast modes of a bimodal
  distribution** with the fast mode appearing for almost any kernel
  with enough trials (014b refinement).
- Warmup recipe: `heavy_write K=1`. Decision 004 inverted on M4 Max.
- Work-dominance: write_tid ≥ 524 K threads, fma_loop ≥ 256 iters.
- Pair timing primary for trials ≥ ~64 µs (decision 005).
- **Sub-floor amplification:** internal-loop with stable-region fit
  for kernels below ~50 µs. Down to ~64-op base units.

#### DVFS state
- 16 GPUPH states: `OFF + P1..P15`. Read sudo-free via
  `notes/ioreport.py --include-states`.
- Per-state MHz mapping (positional, M4 Max specific, exp 012):

      P1 = 338    P6 = 1056   P11 = 1242
      P2 = 618    P7 = 1062   P12 = 1380
      P3 = 796    P8 = 1182   P13 = 1326
      P4 = 924    P9 = 1182   P14 = 1470
      P5 = 952    P10 = 1312  P15 = 1578

  **Peak GPU: 1 578 MHz.** Mapping NOT monotonic in P-index.

#### Power-controller modes (4 known states)
- **`IDLE_OFF`** — between dispatches.
- **`PERF`** — normal sustained execution.
- **`DEADLINE`** — engaged when `(FMA_ITERS ≤ 16384) AND (sleep_us
  ≤ 50)` (exp 013); cycles peak↔idle on a sub-250 ms timescale.
  DEADLINE residency 26-51 % when engaged; chip ping-pongs.
- **`PRFBOOST`** — trigger model **refined by 016**: engages on
  *sustained dispatch activity* with high-enough power draw, not
  specifically memory-bound. 015 saw it on memory-bound ≥ 5 ms;
  016 saw it on compute-bound 128³ matmul at ~2 ms. The exact
  trigger boundary is open. Pegs P15 residency at 93-97 % when
  engaged.
- **PWRCTRL mode and GPUPH P15 residency are independent
  dimensions** (unchanged).

#### Reference per-iter / per-FLOP costs (running DVFS regime)
- Synthetic compute (014b fma_loop carry chain): **6.33 ns / FMA
  per thread**.
- Synthetic memory-latency (015 chase, SLC-defeating table):
  **341 ns / chained DRAM load**.
- Memory-vs-compute synthetic ratio: **53.9×**.
- **Naive fp32 matmul plateau (016, M ∈ [128, 768] square):**
  **0.45-0.54 ps/FLOP ≈ 1.86-2.21 TFLOP/s** at running DVFS.
  ~7 % of fp32 peak.
- **Naive fp32 matmul memory-bound aspect (016, narrow-output):**
  ps/FLOP elevated 3-9× over compute plateau as M shrinks toward
  the GEMV regime.

## Standard experiment template (unchanged)

For amplification or DVFS-characterization experiments:
- Cell duration target: 5 s wall-clock.
- Trial cap: 5 000 per cell (Metal counter sample buffer is 32 768 B
  = 4 096 slots = 2 048 trials, so resolve in chunks of 2 000 to
  reuse the buffer within a cell).
- Inter-cell idle: 1 s.
- Trials at sleep_0 within cell.
- IOReport sidecar at 250 ms with `--include-states`.
- Per-trial monotonic_ns recorded for IOReport temporal join.
- Per-cell PWRCTRL/GPUPH classification works for cells ≥ ~600 ms.

**016 added one operational note:** allocate the
`MTLCounterSampleBuffer` *once* at experiment start and reuse
across all cells. Allocating per-cell exhausts the
`MTLCounterErrorDomain` resource pool around 32+ cells (016's
first run died at cell 32 / 247 with "Cannot allocate sample
buffer"). 015's 16-cell experiments never hit this; for any
experiment with ≥ ~30 cells, use the shared-buffer pattern.

## Strategic frame: where the project actually is

**The methodology arc (001-016) is closed.** The lab can now:
- Time any kernel ≥ 50 µs single-shot (001-006).
- Time arbitrary sub-floor kernels via internal-loop amplification
  (014/014b/015).
- Read DVFS / PWRCTRL state per-cell at 250 ms granularity (007-013).
- Place a kernel on the roofline using shape-axis (size/K/aspect-
  ratio) discrimination (016).
- **Apply the methodology to real ML kernels and recover per-FLOP
  cost with R² ≥ 0.99** (016).

What this *doesn't* mean: that the methodology is finished. 016
also identified concrete refinements:
- Internal-loop amp underestimates cache-fit cost on shapes that
  exceed cache. Single-dispatch direct timing on cache-exceeding
  shapes is a separate (and complementary) tool.
- The PRFBOOST trigger isn't fully characterized.
- The methodology fit-quality gate ("R² > 0.99 AND max residual
  < 5 %") is over-strict for parallelism-floor cells.

What it *does* mean: the bottleneck-classification thesis from
`CLAUDE.md` and `UNKNOWNS.md` is no longer untested speculation.
A real ML kernel (naive matmul) was placed on the roofline using
amplification slopes, and the discrimination signal works.

The **next experiment** (whichever one) is now a question about
what we want to learn next, not whether the foundation will hold.

## What we learned about agent-first lab discipline (carry-over + 016 additions)

Carry-over (001-015 lessons hold):
- Per-chip decisions are now the norm.
- Pre-registration before running every experiment held up across
  17 experiments (no temptation to retro-fit).
- Negative results pile up cheaply.
- "Raw before robust" exposes structure (every cell with enough
  trials shows bimodality the small-N pre-regs didn't predict).
- Observation perturbs state — IOReport at 250 ms is the safe
  cadence floor.
- Pre-reg + immediate follow-up (014 → 014b) is a successful
  pattern when the first run reveals a methodology blind spot.

New from 016:
- **The dense-grid pre-reg revision was correct.** First draft was
  4 shapes (pattern-matched to 014/015's grid widths). User pushed
  back: "what's keeping you from sweeping wider?" The honest answer
  was nothing structural — pattern-matching to small grids when the
  experiment's purpose is *finding a transition* misses the point.
  34-shape final grid resolved the regime structure as a curve, not
  a binary. **Lesson:** match grid density to the question, not to
  predecessor experiments.
- **Optimistic time estimates matter.** Initial estimate was 20-25
  min; actual was ~5 min because most cells hit the 5 000-trial cap
  in under 1 s. Faster runs mean denser grids are even cheaper than
  modeled. Worth being more generous with grid density by default.
- **Multi-cell experiments need shared sample buffers.** 015's per-
  cell allocation pattern doesn't scale past ~30 cells. Caught at
  cell 32 / 247 in the first 016 run. One-line fix.
- **The methodology PASS gate from a pre-reg may need a writeup-
  level reinterpretation.** "R² > 0.99 AND max residual < 5 %"
  read clean during pre-reg but failed parallelism-floor cells
  where intercept dominates and tiny residuals look big in percent.
  Calling 016 "PASS-with-asterisk" rather than retro-tightening
  the criterion preserves the pre-reg's epistemic honesty while
  letting the experiment's substantive finding stand.
