## Operational rules per chip

### M1 Pro / `applegpu_g13s`

- Dispatch-overhead floor: ~8 µs back-to-back.
- Warmup recipe: `same K=1` (avoid `fma_loop K=1`). Decision 004.
- Work-dominance: write_tid ≥ 131 K-262 K threads, fma_loop ≥ 256 iters.
- **No state-channel telemetry validated here yet.** All post-008 work
  on M4 Max only.

### M4 Max / `applegpu_g16s`

#### Timing
- Dispatch-overhead floor: **~6.4 µs cool / ~1.7 µs DEADLINE-mode sub-floor.**
- Warmup recipe: `heavy_write K=1` (avoid `same K=1`). Decision 004 inverted.
- Work-dominance: write_tid ≥ 524 K threads, fma_loop ≥ 256 iters.
- Pair timing primary for trials ≥ ~64 µs (decision 005). Cross-session
  stable to ≤ 0.6 % at 30 min (exp 006).

#### DVFS state
- 16 GPUPH states: `OFF + P1..P15`. Read sudo-free via
  `notes/ioreport.py --include-states`.
- Per-state MHz mapping (positional, M4 Max specific):

      P1 = 338    P6 = 1056   P11 = 1242
      P2 = 618    P7 = 1062   P12 = 1380
      P3 = 796    P8 = 1182   P13 = 1326
      P4 = 924    P9 = 1182   P14 = 1470
      P5 = 952    P10 = 1312  P15 = 1578

  **Peak GPU: 1 578 MHz.** Mapping is NOT monotonic in P-index
  (P10 > P11 in MHz, P12 > P13, P8 = P9). See exp 012.

#### Power-controller modes
- 4 PWRCTRL states observed: `IDLE_OFF`, `PERF`, `DEADLINE`, `PRFBOOST`.
- DEADLINE entry rule (exp 013): engages when **`(FMA_ITERS ≤ 16 384)
  AND (sleep_us ≤ 50)`.** Heavy kernels (FMA_ITERS = 65 536) keep
  PERF regardless of inter-dispatch sleep; ≥ 200 µs sleep drops out
  of DEADLINE for any kernel size.
- DEADLINE residency 26-51 % when engaged; chip cycles peak ↔ idle.
  Counter-intuitive: sleep_50 µs has *higher* DEADLINE residency
  than sleep_0.
- PWRCTRL mode and GPUPH P15 residency are **independent dimensions.**
  Chip can be PERF + sustained P15 (heavy kernel back-to-back), OR
  DEADLINE + cycling P15 (small kernel, sleep_50 µs), OR PERF + low
  DVFS (any kernel + ≥ 200 µs sleep).
- `PRFBOOST` enumerated but never engaged in our recipes; trigger
  unknown.

#### Sub-floor regime
- Recipe: `fma_loop K=20 sleep_0` with FMA_ITERS=1024, write_tid 32 t
  measured. Per exp 009: STRONG REPRODUCE 5/5 across 5 fresh
  subprocesses with 5-7 s gaps between.
- Onset: trial idx 24-33 (consistent across reproductions).
- Min observed: **1 625 ns = exactly 39 ticks** of the 24 MHz
  timestamp clock. At 1 578 MHz peak, this is ~2 564 GPU cycles per
  kernel.
- Cross-attempt deepening real but **not preserved in observed
  PWRCTRL/GPUPH state.** Inter-attempt gaps reset to baseline yet
  next attempt enters faster. Persistence mechanism unknown.

## Telemetry stack

Two collectors, both write CSV with `time.monotonic_ns()`:

| tool | location | sudo? | what it gives | when to use |
|------|----------|------:|---------------|-------------|
| `gpu_telemetry.py` | `notes/` | yes | powermetrics-derived: GPU active residency, freq, power, thermal | absolute power claims with tight tolerance; cross-validation |
| `ioreport.py` | `notes/` | **no** | libIOReport-derived: 11 power buckets + per-state residency for any STATE-format channel | default for any new experiment |

`ioreport.py` adds `--include-states` (since exp 010) for per-state
residency. Default filter: `--state-groups "GPU Stats"` (covers GPUPH,
BSTGPUPH, GPU_SW, PWRCTRL, AFRSTATE, etc., 11 channels). Pass `ALL` to
include other groups (huge CSV warning).

**Standard cadence: 250 ms.** 100 ms perturbs the system being
measured (exp 011 v1, 2/5 reproduce vs 5/5 at 250 ms).

Subprocess pattern unchanged from exp 008: launch ioreport.py from
inside run.py, SIGINT at end for clean flush.

## Strategic frame: where the project actually is

The 001-013 arc has built **substantial foundational measurement
infrastructure** (timing methodology, sudo-free DVFS observability,
power-controller characterization, MHz mapping). Each experiment
fed forward into the next; mechanism understanding compounded
cleanly.

But the project's stated mission (per `README.md`, `CLAUDE.md`,
`UNKNOWNS.md` "Methodology") is **bottleneck classification on real
kernels via controlled microbench perturbations.** That thesis is
entirely untested. Every kernel timed so far is `write_tid` (trivial
memory write) or `fma_loop` (arithmetic-only synthetic).

The dispatch-overhead floor (~6.4 µs cool, ~1.7 µs DEADLINE) sits
awkwardly in the small-kernel regime that practical ML workloads
inhabit (decoder-side single-token, LayerNorm slices, small attention
scores). Without an amplification methodology, single-shot timing
limits us to kernels ≥ ~50 µs, which excludes a lot of what we'd
want to study.

**Loop amplification + two-point timing is the standard answer**
to this resolution-vs-scale problem (`nvbench`, BabelStream, LBL
roofline tooling). Validating it on this hardware is the bridge
between the foundation we've built and the discrimination thesis.

## Most useful unanswered questions

Ordered by **mission relevance**, not by interest:

1. **Does loop amplification + two-point timing recover per-iteration
   kernel cost reliably on Apple Silicon?** Pre-registered as exp
   014. Tests the technique on a controlled fma_loop base unit at
   8 N values with both internal-loop and back-to-back amplification
   methods. Without this, the discrimination thesis stays theoretical.
2. **Does the methodology extend to memory-bound kernels?**
   Conditional on 014 passing. STREAM-style pointer-chase base unit
   amplified the same way. Tests whether the technique works for
   both halves of the roofline.
3. **Can controlled perturbations on a real ML kernel (small matmul,
   small attention) discriminate its bottleneck class?** The
   actual discrimination test. Conditional on 014 + memory-bound
   variant passing.
4. **Cross-chip: does GPUPH/PWRCTRL telemetry work on M1 Pro?**
   Re-running the post-008 stack on M1 Pro is one focused
   experiment. Required before any cross-chip claims about
   bottleneck classification.
5. **External sanity check: peak FLOPS and memory bandwidth
   matching Philip Turner's published M1 Pro numbers.** Untested.
   Cheap; would catch any silent timing-methodology bug.

Lower priority — interesting but not load-bearing:

6. **What carries cross-attempt deepening across PWRCTRL/GPUPH
   gaps?** (009/011) — likely thermal or driver-side state.
7. **Half-life of the M4 Max sub-floor state.** (009)
8. **What triggers `PRFBOOST`?** (013) — never engaged in our
   recipes.
9. **Why is GPUPH index ordering non-monotonic in MHz?** (012)
10. **Why is sleep_50 µs more DEADLINE-friendly than sleep_0?**
    (013) — counter-intuitive but doesn't gate downstream.
11. **+13/+21 µs step at fma_loop iters 192→256.** (004)
    Compiler/microarch threshold.

## What we learned about the agent-first lab discipline (carried over from 0428)

- Per-chip decisions are now the norm.
- Pre-registration before running every experiment held up across 13
  experiments; no temptation to retro-fit any of them.
- Negative results pile up cheaply when pre-registered (exp 007's
  ioreg falsification, exp 011 v1's observation-effect finding).
- "Raw before robust" continues to be the right default — every
  experiment exposed something the pre-registered analysis didn't
  predict, and the raw data made the surprises legible.
- New from 010-013: **observation perturbs state.** IOReport at
  100 ms cadence prevented sub-floor entry in 60 % of attempts;
  250 ms is the safer default. The lab-discipline analogue of 003's
  "calibration probe is itself warmup."
